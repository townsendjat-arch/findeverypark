Top Picks on the Front Range

A few parks worth starting with across Aurora, Centennial, Parker, and Lone Tree.

Aurora

  • Central Park Best Playground

    Destination-style family park with standout climbing play, two playgrounds, and room to spread out.
  • Great Plains Park Best for Toddlers

    Sprayground, open space, and an easy family layout.
  • Cherry Creek State Park Best Overall

    Big trails, water, wildlife, and room to spread out.
  • Red-Tailed Hawk Park Best Inclusive Playground

    One of the most thoughtful inclusive play spaces in the area.
  • Utah Park Best Local + Pool

    Neighborhood park feel with a major indoor pool bonus.

Centennial

  • Centennial Center Park Best Overall Playground

    Big signature park with splash play and strong all-around family appeal.
  • Willow Creek Park Best Trail + Playground

    Great combo of play area, open space, and trail access.
  • deKoevend Park Best Shade

    Mature trees and a relaxed setup make this a strong picnic park.

Parker

  • O’Brien Park Best Downtown

    Walkable, active, and tied right into downtown Parker.
  • Discovery Park Best Year-Round

    Splash in summer, skating in winter, and events in between.
  • Salisbury Park Best Sports + Trails

    Big multi-use park with room for sports and trail access.

Lone Tree

  • Sweetwater Park Best First Stop

    A simple starting point for getting your bearings in Lone Tree.
  • Promenade Park Best Central Pick

    One of the easiest Lone Tree parks to use as a map-and-card anchor.
  • Lonesome Pine Park Best Early Favorite

    A strong Lone Tree park to keep in the first-wave shortlist as coverage expands.
